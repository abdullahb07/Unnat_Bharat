Packed Red Blood Cells

Packed Red Blood Cells Plasma Platelet
What is it?
Blood Collected straight from the donor into a blood bag and
mixed with an anticoagulant is called as whole blood. This
collected whole blood is then centrifuged and red cell, platelets
and plasma are separated. The separated Red cells are mixed with
a preservative to be called as packed red blood cells. 		What is it?
Plasma is the clear yellowish fluid component of blood
that holds the blood cells in suspension. It is also known
as the liquid part of blood. It is composed of 90%
water and contains proteins, electrolytes, and
dissolved gases. 		What is it?
Platelets are small, irregular, colorless cells
that help stop bleeding. They are much smaller
than red or white blood cells and are made in the
bone marrow. Platelets stick together to form a
plug that helps seal up a blood vessel.
User For?
Correction of severe anemia in a number of conditions and blood
loss in case of child birth, surgery or trauma settings. 		User For?
Help to fight infections, maintain the body's
fluid balance and to help with blood clotting. 		User For?
Treatment of bleeding, and to help prevent
bleeding in people who are undergoing surgery.
Lasts For?
Red cells can be stored for 42 days at 2-6 degree celsius. 		Lasts For?
Plasma can be stored for up to a year at
-18 degree celsius. 		Lasts For?
Platelet can be stored for 5 days at
20-24 degree celsius.
